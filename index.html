Stephanie L. Johnson

Postdoctoral fellow in Biochemistry and Biophysics

University of California, San Francisco

I am a biochemist and single-molecule biophysicist studying how the physical structure of genomic DNA controls its regulation.

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow at the University of California, San Francisco, in Geeta Narlikar’s lab, where I am using a combination of ensemble biochemical methods and single-molecule FRET to study chromatin remodeling. I received my Ph.D. at the California Institute of Technology in 2012 with Rob Phillips, where I used the single-molecule technique of tethered particle motion to study the sequence dependence of DNA flexibility and its role in bacterial transcriptional regulation. Before that I was an undergraduate at Stanford University where I worked with Steven Block using optical traps to study molecular motors.

I am also an avid runner and cyclist; the banner image was taken from one of my favorite rides in the Marin headlands.
